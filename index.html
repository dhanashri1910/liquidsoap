Project Information

Project Abstract

This project focuses on the preparation of liquid soap using treated used cooking oil through the process of saponification. Waste cooking oil was first purified using activated charcoal and bleaching earth to remove impurities, odor, and color. The cleaned oil was then reacted with potassium hydroxide (KOH) to form soap paste, which was further diluted with water. Additives such as glycerin, aloe vera extract, and fragrance were incorporated to enhance moisturizing properties and appearance. The prepared liquid soap was evaluated for smoothness, viscosity, and usability. This project demonstrates an economical and eco-friendly method of recycling used cooking oil into a useful hygiene product while reducing environmental waste.

Problem Statement

Commercial liquid soaps contain unknown additives and fragrances that make them unsuitable for laboratory testing and cause unstable pH when mixed with chemicals. This project aims to prepare a simple, eco-friendly liquid soap with controlled composition and stable pH for experimental use.

Objectives

  • To recycle used cooking oil for liquid soap production.
  • To understand the saponification reaction using KOH.
  • To prepare liquid soap using simple laboratory techniques.
  • To improve soap quality using charcoal purification and additives.
  • To evaluate the consistency and usability of the prepared soap.

Methodology

  1. Take used cooking oil and heat it lightly.
  2. Add activated charcoal to the oil, stir well, and keep it aside for a few hours.
  3. Filter the oil to remove dirt and bad odor.
  4. Heat the filtered oil again and add bleaching earth powder.
  5. Leave the mixture undisturbed, then filter to obtain clean oil.
  6. Carefully dissolve potassium hydroxide (KOH) in distilled water.
  7. Add the purified oil to this KOH solution and mix well.
  8. Stir the mixture for about 30 minutes until a thick paste is formed.
  9. Keep the soap paste aside for two days.
  10. Add a small amount of hot water to the paste and mix thoroughly.
  11. Add distilled water, glycerin, aloe vera extract, and fragrance.
  12. Stir continuously until the soap becomes smooth and liquid.
  13. Leave it for some time to obtain ready liquid soap.

Conclusion

Liquid soap was successfully prepared from used cooking oil by purification and saponification using potassium hydroxide. The addition of glycerin and aloe vera improved moisturizing properties, while activated charcoal enhanced oil quality. The final product showed acceptable viscosity and smoothness. This project proves that waste cooking oil can be effectively converted into useful liquid soap, promoting sustainability and cost-effective hygiene solutions.

Industrial Manufacturing Workflow

01

Oil Pre-Treatment & Filtration

Cooking oil is transferred into a settling tank and heated to 60‚Äì70¬∞C. Activated charcoal is added and mixed slowly for 3‚Äì4 hours to remove odor, color, and impurities. The treated oil is then passed through a filter press to obtain clean, clarified oil ready for processing.

02

Preparation of Alkali Solution

Potassium Hydroxide (KOH) is slowly dissolved in distilled water inside a stainless steel tank. This reaction generates heat, so cooling water is used to keep the temperature below 60¬∞C. The solution is mixed until fully clear and uniform.

03

Saponification (Soap Formation)

The purified oil is transferred into the main reactor and heated to 75‚Äì80¬∞C. The prepared KOH solution is added gradually while continuous mechanical mixing is applied. This process is maintained for 90‚Äì120 minutes, during which the oil reacts with KOH to form liquid soap paste.

04

Dilution and Enrichment

Distilled water is added slowly to the soap paste to reach the required volume and consistency. Glycerin and Aloe Vera extract are then introduced to improve moisturizing properties. Activated charcoal slurry is blended for deep cleansing performance.

05

pH Adjustment

The pH of the soap is continuously monitored. Diluted Hydrochloric Acid (HCl) is added gradually to neutralize excess alkalinity and achieve a skin-safe pH range of 8.5‚Äì9.5.

06

Cooling and Fragrance Addition

The finished liquid soap is cooled to 30‚Äì35¬∞C using a heat exchanger. Perfume is added at this stage and mixed evenly to ensure uniform fragrance distribution.

07

Final Filtration and Quality Check

The product is passed through fine filters to remove any remaining particles. Quality tests are conducted, including pH, appearance, viscosity, and foam stability, to ensure consistent product standards.

08

Filling and Packaging

The finished soap is transferred to storage tanks and filled using automatic filling machines into various container sizes such as 500 ml, 1 L, 5 L, or 20 L. Containers are sealed, labeled, and packed for distribution.
